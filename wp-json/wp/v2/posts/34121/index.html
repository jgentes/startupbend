The Bend Venture Conference has announced the finalists for the Social Impact and Growth Stage tracks. The first is a new track aimed at for-profit companies with business models that include a social or environment mission.\u00a0The winning startup could receive an investment between $75,000 and $150,000. The Social Impact Track competition will take place on Thursday, Oct. 13.<\/p>\n

The startups competing are:<\/p><\/div>\n

\n
    \n
  • Hemex Health<\/a> (Portland, Ore.): Improves millions of lives in underserved countries by providing new diagnostics for malaria and sickle cell disease where there’s currently no viable test available.<\/li>\n
  • OpConnect<\/a> (Portland, Ore.): Sells and operates smart electric vehicle charging stations.<\/li>\n
  • Sudara<\/a> (Bend, Ore.): Designs and manufactures beautiful products in India, sold via e-commerce, providing jobs for survivors of sex-trafficking.<\/li>\n
  • TryECO<\/a> (Portland, Ore.): Develops super absorbent polymers that are soil amendments and seed coatings, which increase crop yields with less water while improving soil tilth and biologics.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/div>\n

    Next up are the Growth Stage finalists. These companies have a\u00a0proven concept, have generated initial revenues and are prepared to scale quickly with investment. The winner could receive up to $250,000 in investment. This and the early stage competition takes place on Friday, Oct. 14. The competing startups are:<\/p><\/div>\n

    • Outdoor Project<\/a> (Portland, Ore.): The fastest growing media platform in the U.S. for outdoor recreation and travel.<\/li>\n
    • RFPIO Inc.<\/a> (Portland, Ore.): SaaS solution that streamlines the RFP response process and increases the win rate through automation, collaboration and Salesforce integration.<\/li>\n
