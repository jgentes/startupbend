Alex Eakin, founder of Party Gorilla, has always traveled a lot for work. He often finds himself in new cities, looking for a restaurant or bar that offers a good vibe and lively crowd in addition to great food and drinks. Sure, there\u2019s apps that review restaurants and others that tell you the nearest happy hour. But Eakin realized he wanted a real-time assessment of the fun being had at any given establishment. That idea was the beginning of Party Gorilla, an app that tells you where everyone is hanging out right now<\/em>. BendTECH sat down with Eakin last week to learn more about Party Gorilla, what it does and … why a gorilla.<\/p>\n

Startup:<\/strong> Party Gorilla<\/a>
\nFounded:<\/strong>\u00a0 2014
\nLeadership team:<\/strong> Alex Eakin, founder and CEO; Kyle Smeback, vp of UI\/UX design; Sam Warfield, vp app development; Jameson Strocsher, vp of web development
\nHeadquarters:<\/strong> Bend
\nEmployees:<\/strong> 6<\/p>\n

Can you explain Party Gorilla?<\/strong> We\u2019re a real-time social application that gives bar owners and patrons the ability to share the current atmosphere of bars, nightclubs and restaurants around you. You can use Yelp, FourSquare or Google to find places that people have already been to, but Party Gorilla tells you what\u2019s a happening spot right now.<\/p>\n

How does it do that?\u00a0<\/strong> You download app, and then for instance, you can see all the places with live events, happy hours or just where you\u2019re people are checking in around you. People at the establishment can use our features to rate things such as the energy level, whether it\u2019s crowded or the ratio of men to women. They can also give the place an overall rating, so you can see the cumulative opinion of people who\u2019ve been there.<\/p>\n
