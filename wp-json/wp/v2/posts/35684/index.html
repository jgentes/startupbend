The Bend Chamber of Commerce is organizing the first-ever Bend Young Professional Summit<\/a>, scheduled for Saturday, Sept. 29th. The summit gives professionals ages 21 through 40 the opportunity to network, share ideas and get involved with the community.<\/p>\n

The day-long conference features 10 breakout panels (attendees can choose four) focused on everything from challenges facing the city to how to negotiate a raise to tips for resolving conflict. Two keynote speakers will open and close the event, and then — yes, there’s more — you can head over to an afterparty at Loge Entrada.<\/p>\n

It’s a day packed with learning, connecting and fun. Some of the highlights include the opportunity to:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Get tips and tools for negotiating your next raise from Humm Kombucha’s HR Director Nancy Gammond-Moody.<\/li>\n
  • Learn about Bend’s toughest social issues from Chief of Police Jim Porter and three other frontline leaders on the forefront of tackling crime, mental health, homelessness, addiction and more.<\/li>\n
  • Ask advice from fellow YP entrepreneurs\u00a0who are turning their part time passion projects into full time ventures including\u00a0Ryan Holmes of\u00a0SnoPlanks<\/a>, Mckenna Dempsey of\u00a0Mandala Mission<\/a>, Graham Zimmerman of\u00a0Bedrock Film Works<\/a>,\u00a0Wallis Levin of\u00a0Loloma Lodge<\/a>\u00a0and others.<\/li>\n
  • Learn how to navigate and resolve personal conflict from Hank Queen, former EVP of Boeing who successfully\u00a0led over 40,000 engineers through the biggest white collar strike in U.S. history.<\/li>\n
  • Jump into a creativity lab with local conceptual artist Heather Laurie, creative genius behind the Deschutes Halloween Warehouse Party, Burning Man’s Red Nose theme camp and more.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Um can I go to all of those? If you’re interested, the time to sign up is now.<\/p>\n
