As we blogged here back in July<\/a>, last year’s BVC concept stage winner and aquaponics startup\u00a0Volcano Veggies<\/a> was in the running for the Wells Fargo Works Project. On the line was $26,000 in cash and mentorship from Wells Fargo advisors, catered to the winning company’s business needs.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Volcano Veggies prevailed as one of the five grand-prize winners<\/a>, out of 4200 entries. \u201cWells Fargo would like to congratulate Volcano Veggies LLC on being named a grand prize winner,\u201d says Tracy Curtis, Wells Fargo\u2019s Oregon regional president. \u201cWe were thrilled to again engage business owners across the country through the contest, and we enjoyed hearing their inspirational stories of entrepreneurship. Working with small business owners is one of the most important things that we do, and we are committed to helping them create a path for long-term financial success through a wide range of tools and resources.\u201d<\/p>\n

The startup<\/a> is expanding to a larger space in downtown Bend this fall, adding more modules and products. Bendites will soon be able to join their new year-round\u00a0CSA subscription<\/a>\u00a0with expanded options including spinach, strawberries, tomatoes, micro greens, and they’re gearing up to offer\u00a0super clean and sustainable fish in the near future.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n
