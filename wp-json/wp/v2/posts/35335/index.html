We survived January aka the month that lasts 10 years. In celebration I wanted to relay to you a story that I found so delightful I wish I could hear it again for the first time. I\u2019ve been thinking\u2014and laughing\u2014about this anecdote that Matt Smith, founder of Tribe Pilot, told me for a few weeks.<\/p>\n

IMHO not only was it very entertaining, but I also believe that buried not too deep in this tale is a lesson that startup founders, potential founders and the rest us can draw on when we’re having a hard time making a big leap. More on that in a minute, but first\u00a0Matt\u2019s story \u2013 in his words:<\/p>\n

\n

\u201cI was snorkeling in Australia. The boat had stopped and we were in really deep water, the kind where you can\u2019t see anything except blue. I\u2019m looking down and all of a sudden I see a thing swimming up from the deep. It was moving pretty fast and coming right at me. I realized it was a whale. Then I see that it has its eyes closed and it\u2019s going to hit me. Or maybe eat me.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

Pause for a minute to contemplate\u2014a whale swimming at you with its eyes closed. Back to Matt \u2026<\/p>\n

\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t know what to do. So I just yelled underwater as loud as I could. I saw it open a giant eye, see me and then veer away.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

As it turned out the whale was a baby and probably still learning its way around the ocean. Matt notes that its mom was following close behind\u2014undoubtedly yelling in whale at her baby to stop swimming around with its eyes closed.<\/p>\n
