Smartwaiver, a Bend tech company that specializes in digital liability waivers, announced today that its been acquired. The move provides an exit for co-founder and CEO Mark Silliman, who started the company four years ago. He says that\u00a0Smartwaiver<\/a> will remain in Bend, however, the company will have new leadership.<\/p>\n

Silliman posted this on the company’s blog<\/a>:<\/p>\n

Today I\u2019m excited to announce that Smartwaiver has received a new round of investment that will enable the company and the product to soar to that next level! \u00a0With this investment, a new, seasoned executive and development team will be coming on board and I will be passing the torch to a new CEO.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

Tod Bloxham will be the company’s new CEO. He\u00a0is a 20+ year veteran in the outdoor industry and one of Smartwaiver’s first customers. \u00a0Tod will oversee the business operations and guide the company\u2019s ambitious expansion plans, Silliman says.<\/p>\n

Smartwaiver\u2019s new CTO is\u00a0Ted Knudsen, a developer with 20+ years programming for startups and Fortune 1000 companies. Ted will be in charge of development, infrastructure and feature enhancements.\u00a0Smartwaiver’s Co-founder\u00a0Andy Laakmann will continue to act as technical and business advisor.<\/p>\n

Silliman says he’ll work with the company for another few months and then move onto to other ventures. He declined to provide additional details due to NDAs. Congrats Smartwaiver!<\/p>\n
