As Eating Season — otherwise known as the holidays — rapidly approaches, it seems only fitting that our next #50startups incorporate both food and technology. Lisa and Jim Sipe created the FoodFu cooking competition app after playing their own version of Chopped with their family over Christmas. The app launched in August and now the Sipes are focused on expanding the FoodFu brand with new cocktail and plating apps.<\/p>\n

StartupBend recently caught up with the pair to learn more about their experience in tech, why they decided to leave their previous jobs to pursue FoodFu, and the secret to successfully starting a business with your spouse.<\/p>\n

Startup:<\/strong> Binary Star Systems, creators of FoodFu<\/a> and FoodFu Cocktails
\nFounded:<\/strong> 2015
\nFounders:<\/strong> Jim and Lisa Sipe
\nEmployees:<\/strong> 2
\nHeadquarters:<\/strong> Bend<\/p>\n

How do you describe FoodFu? <\/strong><\/p>\n

Jim:<\/strong> It\u2019s an app that facilitates a cooking competition (like Chopped or Top Chef) in your own home.
\nLisa:<\/strong> It guides you through the competition. You need four players, two to be chefs and two to judge and moderate. After choosing an ingredient, you have 45 minutes to prepare a dish. During that time the app will give the moderator questions to ask the chefs. The judges taste the dishes and the app tallies the scores and announces the winner.<\/p>\n

How did you come up with it? <\/strong><\/p>\n

Lisa: <\/strong>We were at a family gathering over the holidays and we made and played our own version of Chopped. We had so much fun that we were still talking about it a few weeks later. We realized that we could create an app that could facilitate the same experience for others.<\/p>\n
