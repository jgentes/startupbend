Cascade Angels Fund 2015, LLC announced today a $100,000 investment in Lumen Learning, a Portland-based startup that\u00a0helps higher education and K-12 institutions use open educational resources to eliminate textbook costs, broaden access to educational materials, and improve student success.<\/p>\n

The investment was part of a $2.5 million seed round led by the Oregon Angel Fund. It also included the Portland Seed Fund, Alliance for Angels and others.<\/p>\n

This is the first investment by the Cascade Angels 2015 fund, which recently closed with $725,000. \u00a0From the news release:<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are pleased to have Cascade Angels participate,\u201d said Kim Thanos, CEO of Lumen Learning<\/a>. \u201cAccess to Oregon-based capital and expertise is critical to our success in expanding our operations across North America and bringing affordable courseware to community college students.\u201d<\/p>\n

Founded in 2012 by Kim Thanos and David Wiley, the company is also a grantee in the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation\u2019s Next Generation Learning Challenge and Next Generation Courseware Challenge programs. The company has also received grant funding from the Hewlett Foundation and the Shuttleworth Foundation.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

Julie Harrelson, CEO of the Harrelson Group and the fund’s manager, says that Cascade Angels is looking forward to reviewing additional Oregon-based companies for investment. The fund is still accepting applications<\/a> from interested companies.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
