BendTECH officially launched its crowdfunding campaign<\/a> this week with an ambitious goal of raising $80,000 to support Central Oregon tech entrepreneurs.<\/p>\n

A program of the nonprofit\u00a0Tech Alliance<\/a>, BendTECH wants to create:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • A dedicated co-working space for techies and startups.<\/strong> BendTECH (the name of the new co-working space, as well as this blog) will be located at 1001 Emkay. The former Forest Service building is currently being renovated into a modern office space.<\/li>\n
  • Programs to help entrepreneurs.\u00a0<\/strong>The space would also provide education programs aimed at helping startups grow.<\/li>\n
  • A giant meetup space for events.\u00a0<\/strong>The unConference, Startup Weekend, Hack Nights. You name it. BendTECH could host it.<\/li>\n
  • A tech news blog.\u00a0<\/strong>Ahem. Hello. That would be this blog, which aims to relay the region’s tech news and happenings to, you know, the whole world. (Have a story? Contact me<\/a>.)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    <\/p>\n

    “There is a lot of activity around startups and tech in Central Oregon right now,” says James Gentes, executive director of Tech Alliance . “We want to provide as much support as we can to the entrepreneurs and startup founders who call Bend home.”<\/p>\n

    The campaign runs until Oct. 1. Because Tech Alliance is a 501(c)3, your donations are tax deductible.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
