There was a full rainbow right over downtown Bend late this afternoon. It seemed fitting given that it felt like it was raining money at the Bend Venture Conference awards ceremony.<\/p>\n

Conference winners earned more than $940,000 in investments and prizes, including side deals that were still being finalized. In fact, according to EDCO, that amount broke the record for angel conferences in Oregon.<\/p>\n

The big checks (literally, those giant, cardboard checks) just kept coming. Even before the winners of BVC were announced, Dino Vendetti with Seven Peaks Ventures doled out $350,000 in investments from the Bend-based venture fund to three launch stage companies.<\/p>\n

“There’s more!” Vendetti told the crowd after handing out Seven Peaks’ first investment.<\/p>\n

Eleven entrepreneurs took the stage over the course of the day to pitch their companies. The startups ranged from outdoor apparel, subscription boxes and indoor gardens to tech, tech and more tech. The morning half-dozen were vying for the $10,000 BendBroadband concept stage prize, while the afternoon featured launch stage companies hoping for a six-figure investment.<\/p>\n
