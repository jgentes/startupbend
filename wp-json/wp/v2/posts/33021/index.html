\nThe first question people ask when you arrive in Central Oregon is \u201cwhat do you do?\u201d because it\u2019s somewhat of a coup to figure out how\u00a0to live in this singularly beautiful, active, and just urban enough town.\u00a0Bend is well on it\u2019s way to becoming a mecca for independent workers and startups because many of the people who work here, or chose to start their businesses here, could go anywhere.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Now because of BendTECH, we no longer need to languish at home with the dishes in the sink, in a noisy coffee shop, or isolated in boring executive suites.\u00a0There\u2019s a small army of successful independent professionals in this town, and coworking is an emerging way to have better work\/life boundaries and community. You get all the perks of working with people, with the freedom\u00a0of working on your terms.<\/a><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/a><\/center>Startups looking for flexibility and resources have long found a home at BendTECH, formerly TechSpace Bend, since 2010 at two locations downtown.\u00a0Our new home at 1001 SW Emkay<\/a> with Stackhouse Coffee<\/a>, Five Talent<\/a>, Pneuma33<\/a>, Kollective<\/a>, and Seven Peaks Ventures<\/a> is a sweet spot for entrepreneurial activity.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Just last week, Sonny of Handsell<\/a> and Shannon of\u00a0Volcano Veggies<\/a> pitched here at the BVC Unconference, while lots of new folks got to check out BendTech for the first time. Every month, we have member happy hours, lunches and events. This Wednesday, Oct 21st, we\u2019ll be hosting a Tech Talk with speakers from The Hartford, Lumbermens, and Karnopp Peterson on protecting your work and customer data. The talk will be brief and there will be pizza and beer, yay!<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Please contact me, Tierney<\/a>, if you’re interested in membership and would like a tour.
