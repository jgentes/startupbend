As avid mountain bikers, Chris Kratsch and Katy Bryce have spent years bike touring and trail building. They\u2019ve long used BOB trailers to carry their stuff. However, when they bought new mountain bikes in 2012, they realized they had a problem: They couldn\u2019t attach the BOBs. Their new carbon bikes had 12 mm thru axles that attached to the wheel of the bike frame, unlike the older, quick release skewers.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe solution was to make a different axle,\u201d Katy says. Chris was working in a machine shop at the time and had a few made up. They asked around to see if their friends would want the new axles (they did). In short order, the couple launched a small website to sell them, thinking that others likely faced the same issue.<\/p>\n

The pair named their endeavor The Robert Axle Project<\/a>, a play on the connection to the BOB trailers. They now sell axles\u00a0in 35 countries, and are planning for some big growth this year.<\/p>\n

Oh that? It’s Switzerland\u00a0calling<\/strong><\/p>\n

\u201cWe had no idea that this would be a full-time business,\u201d Katy says. But the universe of bike touring enthusiasts had other plans. Within just a few months of launching, they received an order from a large bike parts distributor in Switzerland. (Katy, a freelance writer, used her SEO experience to give the site some juice. It worked). The pair recruited their friends to help fill the order, and a business was born.<\/p>\n
