Bend\u2019s startup community has lost one startup — and gained another. Andrea Bouma and Robyn Gelfand, co-founders of Radventure, told us this morning that they\u2019ve officially called it quits on their peer-to-peer outdoor guide company.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe poured a lot of our heart and soul into it, but in the end we felt like we weren\u2019t positioned to win the battle,\u201d Bouma said. \u201cThe question was whether we continue down this path or cut our losses.\u201d<\/p>\n

As we all know startups face myriad of obstacles, from running out of money to finding talent to making sure they have a good product-market fit. In this case, the Radventure co-founders say they had a lot of positive reception to their idea, but ended up hamstrung by something much more technical: the need for Radventure guides to obtain special use permits when taking people onto federal lands.<\/p>\n
