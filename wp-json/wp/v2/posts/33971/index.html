Need a break from work? Do you love watching flying things? The Pacific Northwest Drone National Qualifier Race<\/a> is happening this Saturday (7\/16) right here in Bend. Sixty drone pilots are traveling from all over the West to compete for five spots at the National Drone Racing Championship in New York City in August.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

The pilots will race in 20 small heats, on a windy, one-kilometer track that has gates and flags. To win, the pilots have to complete the course without missing a gate, and put in one of the five fastest times. If this is your jam, then head on out \u00a0to Camp Fraley to watch. Is there cheering? Are there vuvuzelas? I can’t say, but I’m going to find out.<\/p>\n

In the meantime, the news release says that the event is family friendly and free. You can bring chairs, umbrellas and picnic fixings and hang out\u00a0with your kids, who will undoubtedly love it (there’s also roughly a 1,000 percent chance your kids will ask you buy them a drone).<\/p>\n

The doors open at 8 a.m. for competitors and set-up and the races begin at 10 a.m.<\/p>\n
