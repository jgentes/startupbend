The Bend Chamber is kicking off a monthly spotlight on women and entrepreneurship with a panel of Central Oregon women business leaders Tuesday night. But make no mistake–this event is not just for women, it’s for everyone<\/em>. More on that in a minute.<\/p>\n

The Year of Women in Business panel discussion tomorrow evening features some our region’s most inspiring and experienced business leaders, discussing a range of issues regarding growing companies, leadership, how to be successful and more.<\/p>\n

The panelists are:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Patti Moss<\/strong><\/a>, former CEO\/Pres. Bank of the Cascades and Cascade Bancorp (now\u00a0First Interstate Bank<\/a>)<\/li>\n
  • Jamie Danek<\/strong><\/a>, co-founder\/CEO of\u00a0Humm Kombucha<\/a><\/li>\n
  • Talena Barker<\/strong><\/a>, founder\/CEO of newly created\u00a0Mission Limelight<\/a><\/li>\n
  • Corey Schmid<\/strong><\/a>,\u00a0\u00a0Seven Peaks Ventures<\/a>\u00a0investment partner<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    From the Chamber’s news release:<\/p>\n

    \n

    \u201cThis is a great opportunity to hear from some of our most prominent women leaders and mentors who understand the particular challenges that come with being a woman entrepreneur,\u201d said Talena Barker, founder\/CEO of Mission Limelight.<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

    We wholeheartedly agree. The women on this panel are great, and I could fangirl over each of them. (Side note: Stay tuned for a profile of Talena’s startup, Mission Limelight, tomorrow.)<\/p>\n

    Back to who should attend: You should. These panelists will undoubtedly provide insights and wisdom applicable not just to women, but to anyone<\/em> interested in creating a successful, healthy, vibrant and equitable business ecosystem.<\/p>\n

    Especially in startupland, women can face some key challenges, including access to funding. Consider that women receive just\u00a02.97% of venture capital, and\u00a0less than 5% of SBA-backed bank loans. However, the chamber release notes that when they receive capital, they show results. Women-led, venture-backed companies are\u00a0less likely to fail, use\u00a0less capital, and have\u00a012 percent higher annual revenues.<\/p>\n

    So yes the Tuesday panel is women focused, but the conversation is important for the entire community<\/em>. Because the success of our entrepreneurial ecosystem is never a zero sum game. Instead, the more people that find success, the more success we have overall–that’s more thriving businesses, more job opportunities, better job opportunities, and a stronger economy. It’s a rising tide\/ships situation. And understanding more about the barriers that face underrepresented groups puts us all on a path to a better place.<\/p>\n
