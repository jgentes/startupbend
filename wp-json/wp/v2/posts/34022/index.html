I love a good metaphor. So when the team at Craft CMS\u2014a tech company mostly new to Bend\u2014compared one of their primary competitors to a chicken nugget (they\u2019re more of a custom sandwich, made on artisanal bread, with heirloom tomatoes and other delicious things) I had to know more.<\/p>\n

Two-thirds of the team at Craft CMS are new arrivals to town. Founder and CEO Brandon Kelly started Craft\u2019s parent company, Pixel & Tonic, a software development agency specializing in ExpressionEngine add-ons, in San Jose in early 2011. But he quickly realized that he wanted to do something more.<\/span><\/p>\n

\u201cI wanted to do my own CMS,\u201d Brandon says. The company\u2019s CTO Brad jokes that every developer wants to write his or her own CMS\u2014 \u201cIt\u2019s a rite of passage.\u201d<\/span><\/p>\n

In this case, though, that rite of passage evolved into one of the most well-respected CMS products on the market, with the likes of Salesforce, The Associated Press, and Netflix in its client roster.<\/span><\/p>\n

A different approach<\/strong><\/p>\n

Now getting back to the chicken nugget. As Craft team notes, the CMS world primarily exists on the opposing ends of a spectrum. There\u2019s the nearly free, very templated, DIY products such as WordPress (aka chicken nuggets) on one end and very expensive, enterprise-level products on the other.<\/span><\/p>\n
