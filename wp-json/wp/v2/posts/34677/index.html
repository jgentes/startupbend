A group of pretty awesome women have started the Technology Association of Oregon STEAM (science, tech, engineering, arts and math) initiative and aim to kick it off right with a launch party on Thursday, June 1. The vision? TAO STEAM wants to form a network of Central Oregon professionals committed to creating a path for girls and women interested in STEAM careers — and a gender balanced future.<\/p>\n

One of our favorite people, Maggie Hubbell, CEO of Agency Revolution and Chair of the Steering Committee says, \u201cIn the ideal world the term ‘women in STEAM’ wouldn\u2019t exist. But, the reality is this issue does need special attention right now. We want to provide a supportive community that enriches the experience for women currently in STEAM careers and find ways to attract more women into these fields. Central Oregon has a lot of support around this issue and I believe if we work together we can really make a difference.\u201d<\/p>\n

From the news release:<\/p>\n

TAO STEAM\u2019s mission is to unify Central Oregon\u2019s women in the STEAM community with a vision of enabling Central Oregon\u2019s women to reach their full STEAM potential. Goals are to:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Create paths for girls and women interested in STEAM career<\/li>\n
  • Establish a platform for connections, resources and opportunities<\/li>\n
  • Enable ongoing education opportunities for career growth and prosperity<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/blockquote>\n

    The group is inviting the community, businesses, non-profits, and individuals to collaborate and serve as a resource for learning, growth, and outreach, for both women and Central Oregon as a whole. TAO STEAM members will be invited to join and participate in small groups or communities, built around job functions and key areas in STEAM fields.<\/p>\n

    \u201cThis network squares perfectly with the Central Oregon STEM Hub\u2019s vision for cultivating a regional STEM ecosystem. Our core partners promote opportunities and pathways to prepare students for a future driven by STEM; connecting with the community, non-profits, and industry is essential. It all fits together.\u201d said Whitney Swander, Executive Director, Central Oregon STEM Hub.<\/p>\n

    Interested? Come learn more at the launch party and meet some great people.<\/p>\n

    \n5:30 p.m. to 7:30 p.m.
    \nThe Capitol, 90 NW Oregon, Ave.
    \nBend<\/p>\n
