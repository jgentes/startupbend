When it comes to raising\u00a0funds for charity, there are few things more effective than an auction. But when auction items go for less than what their donors hoped\u2014or don\u2019t capture the audience\u2019s attention at all, then everyone misses out.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe were working on fundraising projects and having conversations behind closed doors about how we\u2019re leaving money on the table with auction items,\u201d says Talena Barker, a former nonprofit fundraiser and founder and CEO of Bend-based Mission Limelight<\/a>.<\/p>\n

The biggest challenge? Most charity auction donations come from local donors, which don\u2019t inspire high bidding because they aren\u2019t new or unique to the local audience, Talena says. Thus, packages sell for far less than value, fail to excite guests and leave auction donors less than happy.<\/p>\n

In trying to make the most of every auction, Talena found a creative workaround. She\u2019d call like-minded nonprofits in other places and trade auction items in hopes of garnering bigger donations for them.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe started setting up these informal exchanges,\u201d Talena recalls. It took a lot of legwork, but the results\u2014better auction items bringing in bigger donations\u2014were worth it.\u00a0Talena was certain that someone would turn this good idea into a company.\u00a0Turns out, that person was her.<\/p>\n

A marketplace for good<\/strong><\/p>\n

Talena and Lisa Shropshire, the startup\u2019s Director of Outreach, have 30 years of nonprofit fundraising experience between them. They started Mission Limelight in January 2017, and have been in whirlwind of startup activity since.<\/p>\n
