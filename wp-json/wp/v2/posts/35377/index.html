If you like people, entrepreneurship, tech and tacos — not necessarily in that order — then we may have a job for you. BendTECH is hiring a new community and operations manager. Here is the listing. Please forward along to anyone who think may be a good fit.<\/p>\n

BendTech Community and Operations Manager<\/b><\/p>\n

Do you love meeting people, organizing events, thinking about how to cultivate community and getting your hands into the operations of a thriving, nonprofit coworking business? BendTECH is hiring a community and operations manager.<\/p>\n

In this position, you’ll handle the daily operations of our coworking space. That means onboarding new members, responding to member inquiries, organizing monthly get togethers, managing our member and office wait list, keeping tabs on our monthly budget and marketing our events.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
The work may include ordering the monthly keg and facilitating Taco Tuesdays, implementing new educational programs, developing community partners, tracking member payments and organizing happy hours. In your downtime, you can experiment with fun new ways to serve our vibrant member community of startups, entrepreneurs and remote workers. We know it’s a range of tasks, and the right person will like trying new things, while staying on top of what needs to be done.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
It’s a fun, friendly and much sought after space. To operate its best, we need an organized, self starting, outgoing, go getter. If this sounds like you, we’d love to know more.\u00a0This is an independent contract position for 20 to 30 hours per week. BendTECH is run by a nonprofit board, whose mission is to connect and grow the local startup and tech community through coworking, resources and events.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Interested? Email a cover letter and resume to coworking@bendtech.com<\/div>\n<\/blockquote>\n
Now I’m 1000% biased because I’m on the BendTECH board. But the space is a really special place. It’s filled with interested, fired-up people. I’ve watched businesses start, startups move into offices and then out and into bigger offices. There are remote workers for big, innovative tech companies. There is at least one blogger. And there’s a regular spate of events that happen monthly including happy hours, Taco Tuesdays, lunch and learns and more. It’s growing up (sniff sniff). Let us know if you want to be part of the next stage!<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
