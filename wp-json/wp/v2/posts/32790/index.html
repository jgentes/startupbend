When Dan Vetras learned that his company Kollective would need to move out its Sunnyvale office space, he floated a seemingly radical idea. What if instead of simply finding more expensive space in Silicon Valley, he moved his office — and willing employees — to Bend?<\/p>\n

That idea became reality in July. Kollective will celebrate the grand opening of its new 4,000-square-foot space in the 1001 Tech Center next week<\/a>. And though some startup founders here have faced questions and skepticism about the viability of scaling a tech company outside of a major tech city, Vetras has turned that concern on its head.<\/p>\n

\u201cI realized that managing and maintaining a VC-funded startup in Silicon Valley was becoming cost prohibitive,\u201d he says. “Investors are very\u00a0reluctant\u00a0to spend their money on\u00a0things\u00a0that are not core to running\u00a0the\u00a0company or that appear to be overly inflated by abnormal market demand.”<\/p>\n

For example, he notes that the Bay Area commercial real estate market is dominated by giant tech companies. Kollective was asked to move from its space so the building owner could make room for much larger neighbor. But when Vetras began to look for a similar digs, he faced paying three times what his previous lease had been to be in Sunnyvale or the surrounding area.<\/p>\n

Recruiting employees in direct competition with employers like Apple or Facebook is another challenge. For a cost-conscious startup it’s hard to provide comparable perks (onsite dry cleaning anyone?) while also offering salaries that keep pace with the cost of living in Northern California, Vetras says.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe needed to find a way to get to cash-flow positive faster, and improve the quality of life our employees,\u201d he says. \u201cI realized Bend could be someplace we could go.\u201d<\/p>\n
