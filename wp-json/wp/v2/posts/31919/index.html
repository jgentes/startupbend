If you\u2019ve ever tried running (or yogging) with a child in a stroller, then keep reading. Because this next #50startup company might just blow your mind. Bend-based Kidrunner is aiming to change how people run (and ski and bike and explore) with their kids with its hands-free, pull-style running stroller.<\/p>\n

This past year was a breakout one for the company. Founded by Will Warne, Andre Cardec and Mauricio Mejia in 2011, Kidrunner earned a spot on the concept stage at the Bend Venture Conference. Elite runner Max King, the company\u2019s first athlete-ambassador, won<\/em> a 10K pulling his daughter in a Kidrunner<\/a>. And the company partnered with Bend-based Composite Approach<\/a> to help bring this not-stroller to market by summer. BendTECH recently talked with Warne to learn more about how he came up with the idea and when you can buy one.<\/p>\n
