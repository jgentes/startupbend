In your dreams, you’ve designed the NEXT BIG THING, raised more than you ever hoped on Kickstarter, and life is good. Then, those dreams turn quickly into a protracted maze<\/a> of international commerce, foreign manufacturing pitfalls<\/a>, taxes, port strikes, and the high cost of fulfillment<\/a>. Portland entrepreneur Kevin Bates actually moved to China<\/a> to make Aruboy<\/a> a success. Central Oregon startups may find help closer to home.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

SisTech is a\u00a0state-of-the-art electronics design and assembly\u00a0facility right here in Bend off Empire Avenue.\u00a0Industry veterans Brad and Lorraine Kennedy<\/a>\u00a0founded the company\u00a0in 2009 and have been growing ever since. (Fun fact, they\u00a0almost named it\u00a0BendTech, and\u00a0were the former owners of this domain.)<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are a total product design realization solution\u00a0for electronics products, from prototyping to full production,” says Brad Kennedy.\u00a0SisTech has a 22,000 square foot facility, 16 employees, three production lines, a\u00a0massive inventory of\u00a0electronic components in stock for assembly, and a shipping department.<\/p>\n

In some cases, they even design and produce packaging too, like for XIM4, the mouse and keyboard adapter for game consoles<\/a>.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Brad Kennedy knows overseas manufacturing is almost the default for\u00a0tech companies. However, he’s had many clients test the waters across the Pacific and return to SisTech. “China is not all it’s cracked up to be,” said\u00a0Kennedy. “Is it always going to be more expensive here than in China? Absolutely, but by the time you figure out shipping, duties, taxes, fine print, I think you’ll\u00a0find it’s not much\u00a0more than doing business here.\u00a0We stand by our quality, protect your IP, and ship products as you need them instead of requiring a big upfront order.”<\/p>\n

Local tech company\u00a0PulsedLight<\/a>, a recent\u00a0#50Startups<\/a>, tasked Kennedy and his team with assembling their LIDAR-Lite product, a favorite in the small drone industry.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

“We invest heavily in technology,” says Kennedy. “It\u2019s all about keeping current with the industry, always being able to build anything.\u00a0I invested substantially\u00a0in the last 10 months to keep up and add capacity. For example, a 3D X-Ray allowing for non-destructive failure analysis of electronic assemblies will be\u00a0installed this month.”<\/p>\n

SisTech produces approximately 700 products a year. If you\u00a0have something ready to launch from your garage or local makerspace, SisTech might help you avoid the pitfalls of hardware tech startups, and create a product truly made in Bend.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
