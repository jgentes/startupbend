Bart and Ashleigh Mitchell started Goodkid Foods out of sheer necessity: They were tired of feeding their kids crap. Sugary crap to be exact. The parents of two young kids found themselves relying on snack bars that often seemed more than like dessert than a healthy nosh between meals.<\/p>\n

So Ashleigh began baking. She developed numerous recipes for healthy snack bars before landing on one that her kids devoured. That was 2011. Now you can find Goodkid bars in Nordstrom eBars, at Mt. Bachelor\u2019s cash registers and even Whole Foods. BendTECH sat down with Bart Mitchell to learn more about the couple\u2019s plans for their snack bar startup and to try a few Goodkid bars ourselves.<\/p>\n

Startup: Goodkid Foods<\/a>
\nFounders: Bart and Ashleigh Mitchell
\nStarted: 2012
\nHeadquarters: Bend<\/p>\n

How did you get started?<\/strong> We weren\u2019t happy with the options we had for snack bars for our two kids, now ages 7 and 3. Not only are most of them packed with sugar, but they also often had sports messages on them. We think every kid is different, and the snacks didn\u2019t necessarily need to promote just excelling at athletics. So Ashleigh started to bake her own.<\/p>\n

Does she have a baking background?<\/strong> No, not really. She\u2019s a pilates instructor and self-taught when it comes to baking. She started with her own recipe. They\u2019re mostly almond-butter based. She\u2019d make in them in small batches and we\u2019d eat them. Our own kids and their friends were the first taste testers. They liked them a lot and we realized pretty quickly that we could make a business out of this.<\/p>\n

How do you describe Goodkid bars?<\/strong> They\u2019re really low in sugar — they only have about 5 grams of sugars, which is half the amount of other popular brands. We\u2019re pleased that the flavors don\u2019t sound like desserts. They\u2019re lemon blueberry, oatmeal raisin and apple cinnamon.<\/p>\n
