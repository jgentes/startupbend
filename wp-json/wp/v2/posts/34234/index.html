It\u2019s conventional wisdom that the best products and companies come from people who were trying to solve their own problems. That seems to be the case with Ryan Doherty and Zach Hines, co-founders of Ripclear, a Bend startup that creates (actually invented) adhesive ski goggle protectors<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Ryan was working at Mt. Bachelor as an inventory controller in 2013, basically making sure that the resort\u2019s various lodges had all the food and beverages they needed to serve their customers. An avid snowboarder, he\u2019d often snowboard between the mountain\u2019s lodges and buildings.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was scratching up to three or four pairs of goggles a year,\u201d he says. He thought that there had to be something he could adhere to the front \u2013 like a cellphone screen cover \u2013 that would prevent the damage. But after a lot of Internet searching, he found exactly nothing.<\/p>\n

\u201cSo we decided to make it,\u201d Ryan says. Did he have deep experience in the eyeware industry? No. Was he familiar with plastics or manufacturing? Nope. But like many successful\u00a0entrepreneurs, he and Zach just dug in, made themselves the experts and are now selling RipClear goggle protectors across the country.<\/p>\n
