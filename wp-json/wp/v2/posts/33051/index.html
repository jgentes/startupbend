Sometimes you don’t need to raise six or seven figures to get your project humming. Sometimes a small amount can lead to big change. That’s exactly what’s happening with one of Bend’s more delightful projects right now, Awesome Bend.<\/p>\n

The group awarded Serenity Johnson a $1,000 grant to make her idea of expanding Little Free Libraries (aka the cutest things ever) to the neglected parts of Bend a reality. Johnson was one of five finalists pitching their ideas Tuesday night at the Deschutes Brewery Tap Room. The grant came from ten $100 donations from Awesome Bend trustees.<\/p>\n

“This project [will] promote one of the most valuable things we have\u2014literacy,\u201d said Johnson.\u00a0\u201cWithout reading, we would not be able to do math, drive, shop, learn, and live a functioning life.These libraries [will] not only bring the community closer, but also make the gift of learning\u00a0accessible to each and every person in Bend.\u201d<\/p>\n

Johnson is a senior at Summit High School and just moved to Bend a month ago. Our hats off to her for diving in and making her new home a better place. Johnson accepted the prize from Awesome Bend’s student trustee Olivia Brumwell, a junior at\u00a0Bend High.<\/p>\n
