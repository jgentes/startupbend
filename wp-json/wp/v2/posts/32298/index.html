The Collision Conference<\/a> selects a few, “exciting, disruptive, early-stage startups<\/a>” each year to exhibit for free alongside bigger tech companies, and chose Odysys<\/a> for the 2015 event next month in Las Vegas.<\/a><\/p>\n

Odysys, a digital marketing platform for independent hotels, is currently emerging out of a successful closed beta. Their tech platform offers responsive website templates, a commission-free booking engine, analytics, SEO tools and social media management. \u201cWe\u2019re very pleased to have this opportunity,\u201d said Kent Schnepp, Odysys\u2019 CEO and founder. \u201cThere is no shortage of startups, so to be recognized as something special, especially at this stage, is exciting.\u201d<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Collision\u00a0features sessions by notable tech and marketing figures<\/a>, including Tony Hsieh, CEO of Zappos; Stewart Butterfield, founder and CEO of Slack; and Dave Luhr, president of Wieden + Kennedy.\u00a0An estimated 10,000 people are expected to attend this year.<\/p>\n
