<\/strong>Sometimes things just naturally go together, like peanut butter and jelly or puffy coats and shorts. For Zealios, a formerly Berkeley-based startup that recently relocated to Bend, it\u2019s their line of personal products and triathletes. \u201cThe second we dipped our toe into the triathlete community, we started growing,\u201d says Austin Britts, Zealios co-founder.<\/p>\n

Britts and his co-founder, Kevin Fuller, started Zealios<\/a> in 2009 in a quest to develop a better zinc-based sunscreen — one that\u00a0 was water and sweat proof, lasted through workouts and didn\u2019t turn your face white.<\/p>\n

The pair were coworkers at a chemical trading company in Seattle, and they were intimately familiar with all the ingredients that go into skincare products, the good and the bad. \u201cWe wanted to create a product that performed really well and was really safe,\u201d Britts says.<\/p>\n
