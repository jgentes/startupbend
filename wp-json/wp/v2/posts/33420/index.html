If Bend\u2019s newest wave of tech companies were a high school (mascot: The Skiing Geeks), then Manzama would be an upperclassman. The startup, which makes a business intelligence platform primarily for attorneys, won the Bend Venture Conference six years ago and raised $1.3 million in Series A funding in 2014<\/a>. In the interim, the company has been growing, expanding its product line and experimenting with new verticals. Now CEO Peter Ozolin, who previously founded and sold a company called Legal Anywhere, is focused on profitability. We caught up with Ozolin to learn more about Manzama\u2019s recent progress and future plans.<\/p>\n

Startup:<\/strong> Manzama
\nFounders:<\/strong> Peter Ozolin, Mark Hinkle, Randall Smith
\nEmployees:<\/strong> 26
\nHeadquarters:<\/strong> Bend
\nFounded:<\/strong> 2010<\/p>\n

You made some big changes last year. Can you tell us about that? <\/strong><\/p>\n

Sure. Yes, we\u2019ve been thinking about this idea of bootstrapped-plus, where you raise some money in the early phases and then get off the venture capital bandwagon and never raise any more again.\u00a0 You don\u2019t have to because you\u2019re building a real business, one that is profitable and self-sustaining. So one of the decisions we made last year was that we wanted to make that happen. That said, there\u2019s nothing to suggest this path can\u2019t change: If opportunities arise we could go back into raising capital to grow more quickly. But it\u2019s more about having more than one option as an outcome.\u00a0 Already, there\u2019s evidence that financing for the mid\/later stage is slowing down, which if you are not profitable, could mean changing valuations, etc.<\/p>\n
