TiE Oregon<\/a> announced the finalists for 2016 Pitch Oregon<\/a>\u00a0earlier this week, and the list included two Bend companies. Hydaway and Red Plate Foods will compete in the seed\/bootstrap category at the event March 16th for a chance to win prizes and an overall award of $2,500 to $10,000.<\/p>\n

Hydaway<\/a>\u00a0makes a collapsible water bottle that you can put in your pocket and has been featured as one of our #50startups. Red Plate Foods<\/a> makes allergen-free foods and treats that you may have seen already seen in stores, including Fred Meyer, around Oregon and SW Washington.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

These Bend businesses are two of three competitors in their category. The third is Portland-based TripGrid.\u00a0\u201cWe were overwhelmed by the number of outstanding applications we received and it was very difficult to select the finalists,\u201d said Kari Naone, Executive Director of TiE Oregon, in a news release.<\/p>\n

The finalists were selected from over 70 applications from a variety of industries and from all over the state. Here are the finalists for all three categories:<\/p>\n
