<\/strong>The founding team of AirFit has gotten a lot done since presenting as an early stage finalist at the Bend Venture Conference just three months ago. The startup, which aims to operate gyms inside of airports, opened its prototype facility here in Bend, is planning for a first location in New York\u2019s JFK airport and began raising a Series A round of $2 million. We caught up with AirFit\u2019s Co-founder and Chief Operating Officer Jenna Dodge recently to learn more about the company, what travelers want from airport gyms and AirFit\u2019s plans for the future.<\/p>\n

I guess I\u2019m wondering, why aren\u2019t there gyms in airports already? <\/strong>It\u2019s been tried before, but it was executed poorly. Companies before had located their gyms pre-security, which is inconvenient for travelers. There was one in the Las Vegas airport, but that wasn\u2019t the ideal spot for an airport gym, given that it is a tourist destination airport and most people coming through aren\u2019t looking to workout.<\/p>\n
