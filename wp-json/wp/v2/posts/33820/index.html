By Matt Sybrant, EDCO’s Key Industry Coordinator<\/p>\n

<\/strong>What do you get when you cross a pitchfest, five nonprofits and Leadership Bend? You end up with an event called \u201cPitch for a Purpose.\u201d This Tuesday, May 10th,\u00a0the Central Oregon Impact Summit — a project facilitated by Leadership Bend — is hosting a nonprofit pitchfest.<\/p>\n

Five nonprofits will face off on stage, competing for a grand prize of $10,000. In addition to the grand prize, there will be an audience choice award of $2,500 given to one of the presenters. The five presenting nonprofits are:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Deschutes Children\u2019s Foundation<\/a><\/li>\n
  • Healthy Beginnings<\/a><\/li>\n
  • High Desert Food and Farm Alliance<\/a><\/li>\n
  • LaPine Community Kitchen<\/a><\/li>\n
  • Treehouse Therapies<\/a><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    These five nonprofits have completed a gauntlet of workshops focused on marketing and messaging, business plan development, fundraising strategy and crafting a pitch — all leading up to this one opportunity to compete for the cash.<\/p>\n

    Ken Streater will deliver the keynote address. Streater is an international rafting company owner, social entrepreneur, acclaimed author, successful real estate brokerage partner and community growth innovator. The panel of judges include Jodie Barram, of the Tower Theatre Foundation; Betsy Warriner, from Volunteer Connect; Matthew McCoy, from COCC; Ted Schoenborn from the Bend Parks and Rec; and Julie Gregory from Oregon Community Foundation.<\/p>\n
