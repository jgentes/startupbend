<\/del>I\u2019ve been meaning to catch up with Seven Peaks Ventures for a while, and I finally had the chance. I\u2019m glad I did. The Bend-based VC firm has gone through quite the growth spurt over the past year from moving into new offices to growing its partner network to more than doubling the number of companies in its portfolio.<\/p>\n

Let\u2019s start with the investments. Seven Peaks <\/a>currently has 18 companies in its portfolio, up from eight in 2014. Founded three years ago, many of the firm\u2019s first investments were in Bend startups — Droplr<\/a>, Amplion<\/a>, Manzama<\/a> and Crowdstreet — bringing some crucial early stage capital to what was essentially a funding high desert.<\/p>\n

However, as the VC firm has added partners and areas of expertise it\u2019s also expanded its reach, investing in Portland companies such as Cozy, CrowdSupply, and Opal as well as startups in the Bay Area, Boulder and Salt Lake City. Oregon startups currently account for just over half of the firm\u2019s investments.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve had unique access to companies that are based here in Oregon, where we\u2019ve seen a real need for sophisticated, early stage, institutional investing,\u201d says Investment Partner Corey Schmid. \u201cBut as we\u2019ve grown, we\u2019ve added partners and we\u2019re expanding to be a bridge to VC communities all over the West.\u201d<\/p>\n
