A team of small business experts is hosting a food and beer business bootcamp this Sunday evening, July 19, in Bend.\u00a0The event aims to provide small food producers and craft brewers with a free, 90-minute crash course in crowdfunding, marketing and scalability.<\/p>\n

It’s part of the 10-city Food Biz Road Trip<\/a>. The tour is organized\u00a0by six organizations:\u00a0Small Biz Triage, Hey Chef!, Small Food Business, Crowdfund Better, BreweryResources and The Crowdfunding Centre (UK).<\/p>\n

From the news release:<\/p>\n

\n

\u201cWith the ongoing obsession with tech startups, we\u2019ve seen a gaping hole in biz support for small food producers and craft brewers,\u201d says founder Nate Wright of Small Biz Triage. \u201cAnd with the recent hunger for a human touch in biz, hitting the road and getting in the trenches with these creative entrepreneurs seemed to be the best way to help them out.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cOver $16 billion was raised by crowdfunding in 2014 alone \u2026 and a huge chunk of that funneled into the craft food and beer biz, with successful campaigns in this niche raising an average of $16,000,\u201d says Kathleen Minogue, partner and crowdfunding consultant at Crowdfund Better.<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

Bend is the fourth city in the tour. The bootcamp is scheduled for\u00a06 p.m.<\/span><\/span>\u00a0Sunday at the JEM Tasting Room in NWX. There’s room for 25 owner-operators and spots are still available.<\/p>\n
