George Pierce\u2019s garage looks like combination of a running shoe store and a Franken-shoe workshop. There are shelves filled with dozens of brands of shoes, many of which George has ripped apart, modified and re-engineered all in with the aim of getting rid of the shoelaces.<\/p>\n

Why? \u201cI was losing time during duathlon transitions getting my shoes on and off. I wanted a better way,\u201d George says. His better way \u2013 a Bend startup called Pierce Footwear<\/a> \u2013 provides professional athletes, several local runners and the entire U.S. Special Olympics Winter Team with an innovative lace-less shoe.<\/p>\n

Now George is starting to raise a Series A to ramp production of his shoes and make them even more widely available.<\/p>\n

George\u2019s background is mix of tech, entrepreneurship and professional athletics. Before starting Pierce Footwear in 2013, he spent a decade working in Silicon Valley. He founded a tech company, which gave employers a way to track their employees\u2019 wellness, that was acquired in 2008.<\/p>\n
