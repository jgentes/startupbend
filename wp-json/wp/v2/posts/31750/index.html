Need something warm and fuzzy on this icy morning? I’ve got just the thing. Bend-based BlueBird Strategies<\/a> announced this week that its annual give back campaign<\/a> will support Girls Who Code, a nonprofit aimed at closing the gender gap in the computing fields.<\/p>\n

“By supporting Girls Who Code<\/a>, we hope to help equip the next generation; our daughters, our friends’ daughters, nieces, grandchildren and future children\/grandchildren for success,” says BlueBird Strategies CEO Cari Baldwin. The company’s give back campaign is pretty simple. Here’s how it works:<\/p>\n

BueBird Strategies will donate $1 for:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Every retweet or mention on Twitter about BBS and its Girls Who Code campaign. Use the hashtag: #BBS4GWC. In fact, you can click here to tweet about it now<\/a>.<\/li>\n
  • Each new Facebook fan on the BBS Facebook page<\/a>, or reshare mentioning the BBS Girls Who Code campaign. Just tag BlueBird Strategies or use the hashtag above.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    That’s not too hard. But wait, there’s more. BBS is also giving away an iPad mini or GoPro 3 to encourage donations. To enter yourself to win, just like the BBS Facebook page, retweet\/mention the give back campaign or donate at least $5<\/a>.<\/p>\n

    BlueBird Strategies is a\u00a0marketing automation and demand generation firm that serves fast-growing tech companies, including established firms and venture-backed startups. And Baldwin has been on this very blog before, offering free inbound marketing advice for startups<\/a>.<\/p>\n
