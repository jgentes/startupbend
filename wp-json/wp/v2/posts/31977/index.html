Cascade Angels, an angel fund based in Bend, is accepting applications from Oregon companies seeking investment. The Fund plans to raise a minimum of $300,000 by March 31 to facilitate investments averaging from $50,000 to $200,000, according to a news release.<\/p>\n

The criteria for interested companies includes that they:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Be based in Oregon<\/li>\n
  • Are at launch stage, with a product and revenue<\/li>\n
  • Offer the potential for a healthy return rate of seven to 10x<\/li>\n
  • Are seeking from $50,000 up to $1 million in investment<\/li>\n
  • And are interested in equity investments (that is the Fund’s preference)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    But you can operate in any industry. There’s an additional list of optional criteria you can find here, along with the application<\/a>.<\/p>\n

    Cascade Angels<\/a> raised $460,000 for its previous fund, which made investments in Manzama, Droplr and Amplion, which are all headquartered in Bend, as well as Portland-based Poached Jobs.<\/p>\n

    The Fund is fueled by\u00a0accredited investors from Oregon, Washington, and California.
