Julie Harrelson, CEO of the Harrelson Group, Fund Manager of Cascade Angels, has a beloved penchant for taking selfies with entrepreneurs, investors and people of Oregon who support building a strong Oregon through entrepreneurialism. This blog continues a series of selfies + blogs about the amazing people she\u2019s met in the last couple of years. #julieselfie<\/em><\/p>\n

Recently, I had a wonderful visit with Meg Chun, co-founder and CEO at KIALOA<\/a>, a Bend-based paddle company. Our conversation covered the early days of the company in Hawaii and making paddles on the lanai, moving to Bend and working out of a garage, her international paddle collection and Central Oregon\u2019s growth.<\/p>\n

Where did the idea come from for KIALOA and why Central Oregon? <\/strong><\/p>\n

KIALOA started on Oahu. My husband Dave and I were avid outrigger paddlers and Dave felt that there was a way to improve upon the paddles that we were using.\u00a0He had strong beliefs about paddle design so he started building paddles on our lanai.<\/p>\n

In 1992, after much research, we decided to move to Bend, Oregon because we couldn\u2019t afford to buy a house in Hawaii. We\u2019ve been Bendites ever since.\u00a0 Today KIALOA sells 20,000 paddles a year to outrigger paddlers, SUP paddlers, dragon boat paddlers and kayakers around the world and is regarded as one of the best paddle companies on the planet.<\/p>\n
